An Introduction to German Greetings for Beginners

Learning a new language opens the door to understanding new cultures and societies, and one of the first steps in this journey is often learning how to say hello. For those eager to start speaking German, we’ve compiled a beginner-friendly guide to understanding and using German greetings. Whether you're visiting Germany, Austria, Switzerland, or simply looking to learn some basic German phrases, you'll find this guide quite handy.

1. Saying "Hello"

In German, there are various ways to greet someone depending on the time of day, your relationship with the person, and the region you're in.

Step 11: Learn how to count by hundreds

Learn the German word for 'hundred' : The word for hundred in German is 'hundret' (HOON-dert). Start by memorizing the first few numbers in increments of 100. For example, "einhundert" (IEN-hoon-dert) means 100, "zweihundert" (TSVAI-hoon-dert) means 200, "dreihundert" (DRAI-hoon-dert) means 300, and so on.

Step 12: Continue learning

1. Learn the numbers from 100 to 199: The numbers from 100 to 199 in German are formed by adding "hundert" (hundred) after the number from 1 to 99. For example, "einhundert" (IEN-hoon-tuh) means 100, "einhundereins" (IEN-hoon-tuh-IENS) means 101, and "einhundertneunundneuzig" (IEN-hoon-tuh-NOIN-und-NOIN-tsik) means 199.

2. Learn the words for "thousand" : The word for thousand in German is "tausend" (TAO-zent).

3. Learn the numbers from 200 to 99 : To count from 200 to 999, you need to combine the hundreds with the numbers from 1 to 99 using "und" (and). For example, "zweihundert" (TSVAI-hoon-dert) means 200, "zweihundertdreiundzwanzig" (TSVAI-hoon-dert-DRAI-und-TSVAN-tsik) means 223, and "neunhundertachtundneunzig" (NOIN-hoon-dert-AHKT-und-NOIN-tsik) means 998.

4. Combine hundreds and thousands : To count from 1000 to 9999, you need to combine the thousands with the numbers from 1 to 999 using "und" (and). For example, "eintausend" (IEN-tau-zent) means 1000, "eintausendvierhundertfünfzig" (IEN-tau-zent-FEER-hoon-dert-FUENF-tsik) means 1450, and "neuntausendneunhundertneunundneunzig" (NOINT-tau-zent-NOIN-hoon-dert-NOIN-unt-NOIN-tsik) means 9999.
